# Monetization Models
How a waitlist API generates revenue and what pricing structures align with early-stage SaaS founders.

## Free Tier as Growth Engine
A generous free tier (e.g., 1,000 signups, basic referral tracking) lets founders adopt without friction. The API itself becomes viral — every waitlist page is a distribution channel.

## Pricing Axes
- **Signup volume** — free up to N, then per-signup or tiered
- **Features** — referral tracking, analytics, webhook integrations as paid upgrades
- **White-labeling** — remove "powered by" branding for a fee
- **SLA/support** — priority support for launch-critical moments

## Revenue Timing Problem
Target users are pre-revenue founders. They're price-sensitive NOW but potentially high-value LATER if their product succeeds. This suggests:
- Land with free/cheap during pre-launch
- Expand into post-launch tools (beta invites, onboarding queues, feature gating)
- Capture value when the founder has revenue

## Competitive Landscape
Existing players: LaunchRock, Viral Loops, Waitlist.me, KickoffLabs. Most bundle waitlists into broader landing-page or marketing tools. A pure API-first approach is differentiated but must justify why founders should integrate vs. use a no-code widget.

## Open Questions
- Is API-first a feature or a limitation for non-technical founders?
- Should there be a hosted widget/embed for zero-code integration?
- What's the expansion path after launch day?
